On October 1st 2021 the Fortress smart contract Hub will have its decentralized launch. Bootstrapped by a large group of independent Cosmos ecosystem validators, the Fortress-1 main-net will mark a new chapter for the COSMOS ecosystem and blockchain space at large.

About Fortress

Fortress is a sovereign interoperable smart contract blockchain and part of the Cosmos ecosystem, enabling a simple environment for the deployment of smart contracts. The network serves as a decentralized, permission-less & censorship resistant avenue for developers to efficiently and securely launch smart contracts using proven frameworks and compile them in various languages Rust & Go.

CosmWasm, will allow for decentralized applications (dapps) to be compiled on robust and secure multi-chain smart contracts.

Fortress will launch with the latest Cosmos SDK version 0.4.3. Initially the network will potentially go live without the CosmWasm 1.0 smart contract module. This will allow the ecosystem to focus on a stable launch and prioritize integrations on Emeris, Gravity Protocol and Osmosis AMM. Once CosmWasm 1.0 is in beta, an onchain proposal will introduce the module. If the beta releases before October 1st, Fortress may launch with Wasm. You may track the CosmWasm 1.0 progress here.

Dapps created for FortressHack phase 1 can then be deployed on the mainnet unlocking phase 2 of FortressHack which will introduce another wave of incentives for developers and gives Fortress an early adoption head start. There are currently many developers collaborating on Fortress dapps covering categories such as AMMs, NFT marketplaces, Vaults and more.

Fortress uses a novel incentive structure whereby the difference between the genesis supply (64.5 Mill) and the max supply (185.5 Mill) are fixed in the protocol & allocated to parties securing the network ie. validators & delegators over 12 years based on a fixed yearly amount that gradually approaches the 185.5 Million max supply. During the first 3 years after genesis there are 2 halving events. Once the yearly allocation reaches 10% it gradually reduces on a 1% basis each year. At the end of phase 12 (12 years) the max supply is reached and Fortress becomes fully deflationary.
